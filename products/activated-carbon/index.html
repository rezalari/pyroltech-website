Activated carbon, from renewable biocarbon.

Pyroltech activated carbon is a bio-based filtration and purification media made by steam or chemical activation of our own biocarbon. Activation opens the pore structure to surface areas above 1000 square metres per gram, so it does the same jobs as coal-based and coconut-shell carbons, cleaning water, air and process gas and recovering gold, from a renewable base with a supply chain you can plan around.

How is bio-based activated carbon made?

It starts as biocarbon from our pyrolysis process, already a clean, carbon-rich solid. Activation then develops the internal pore network: steam activation etches the carbon at high temperature, while chemical activation uses an activating agent to open porosity at lower temperatures. Both routes push surface area above 1000 square metres per gram. The choice of route, and of the biomass we start from, is how we steer the balance of micropores and mesopores toward gas-phase or liquid-phase duty.

Why switch from coal or coconut carbon?

Most activated carbon today is made from fossil coal or from coconut shell that travels a long way from a handful of producing regions. Both bases carry risk: coal carbon carries a fossil footprint that buyers increasingly have to account for, and coconut supply moves with harvests and freight. A bio-based carbon made from regional biomass gives procurement a third option: comparable adsorption performance, a renewable and biogenic base, and production that can sit close to demand. For utilities and industrials with published climate targets, it turns a routine consumable into a reported win.

How big is the opportunity?

The activated carbon market is roughly US$5.6 billion today and headed to about $11.9 billion by 2034. The drivers are structural rather than cyclical: tightening drinking-water rules, PFAS treatment programs, air-quality regulation and the growth of biogas upgrading all consume carbon, and a growing share of buyers now specify renewable or low-carbon supply. Bio-based activated carbon sits directly on that demand.

Where is the technology proven?

The biocarbon base is produced on Pyroltech technology that runs today at a 2 MW plant in Turkey, running non-stop, built on more than 20 years of thermochemical engineering across three continents. Activation grades are developed against each buyer's duty and tested before they ship. If you want the operating history, ask us and we will take you through it.

Frequently asked questions

How is it different from coal or coconut activated carbon?

Same job, different base. Ours starts as renewable biocarbon from regional biomass rather than fossil coal or imported coconut shell, so buyers get comparable grades with a lower fossil footprint and a steadier supply chain.

What surface area does it reach?

Above 1000 square metres per gram, via steam or chemical activation, with pore structure tuned to gas-phase or liquid-phase duty.

What is it used for?

Water treatment, air and gas purification, gold recovery in carbon-in-leach and carbon-in-pulp circuits, and industrial process purification.

How big is the market?
