What gases can waste become?

Everything starts from syngas, the hydrogen and carbon monoxide mixture that gasification produces from residues. Cleaned to engine and catalyst grade, that one gas branches into a slate of products, and the branch you take is a commercial decision, not a technical constraint.

How does on-site heat and power work?

A share of the syngas never leaves the fence line. It fuels the plant itself, supplying the process heat and electricity that keep the conversion running, so the facility largely powers itself rather than drawing on the grid. Surplus energy can be exported to the host site or the network. For remote operations, island communities and off-grid industry, that combination, waste disposal plus firm local energy, is often the product that matters most.

Which gas is right for your site?

Follow the offtake. A connection to the gas grid makes renewable natural gas the natural product, injected where the pipeline already runs. A port makes methanol or ammonia attractive, both are tradeable, shippable and increasingly sought as marine fuels. An energy-hungry industrial host points to heat and power. A refinery or fertilizer plant next door points to hydrogen. Pyroltech configures the gas train around the buyers you have, and the same plant can shift emphasis as markets evolve.

Is the platform behind it proven?

The gas train is not new to us. Pyroltech has built waste-to-value plants for more than 20 years across three continents, and operates a 2 MW plant in Turkey, running non-stop. The clean-gas slate is that same platform, configured product by product. Ask us for details.

Part of the syngas fuels the plant itself, so the facility largely runs on its own gas, with surplus energy for the host site or grid.

Which gas product is right for our site?
