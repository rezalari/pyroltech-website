How are nutrients recovered?

Wet wastes are poor fuel but rich fertilizer. Hydrothermal processing handles them as they are, no drying required, converting the organics to energy and carbon products while the mineral nutrients concentrate in the process water and solids. From there, recovery is chemistry: phosphorus is precipitated as struvite, a clean magnesium ammonium phosphate mineral; ammonia nitrogen is stripped from the liquor and captured as an ammonium product; and potassium is recovered from the aqueous stream. What used to leave a treatment plant as a disposal problem leaves ours in bags.

Why does phosphorus recovery matter?

Phosphorus is mined from finite phosphate rock, concentrated in a handful of countries, yet most of what flows through cities and farms is lost after a single use, to landfill, to lagoons, or to rivers and lakes where it feeds algal blooms. Regulators are tightening on both ends, restricting land application of raw biosolids while pressing utilities on nutrient discharge. Recovery answers both at once: the pollutant is removed from the water cycle, and a critical crop nutrient goes back into circulation locally instead of being shipped from a mine.

What is the product worth in the field?

Struvite behaves the way agronomists want fertilizer to behave. It is a slow-release mineral that dissolves as plant roots demand it, so the nutrient feeds the crop across the season rather than spiking and leaching after rain. It is granular, low in odour and predictable to spread, and it carries phosphorus, nitrogen and magnesium together. Blended with our biochar, it becomes a carbon-negative soil product, nutrients and stable carbon in one application.

Who is this for?

On the supply side, wastewater utilities, livestock operations and food processors, anyone paying to haul, spread or lagoon a wet nutrient-rich stream, and facing tighter rules on doing so. On the demand side, fertilizer blenders and growers who want a local, traceable nutrient source. Pyroltech has engineered waste-to-value plants for more than 20 years across three continents, and nutrient recovery is how the wet-waste line earns twice: once on disposal, once on product.

Frequently asked questions

How are nutrients recovered from wet waste?

Hydrothermal processing concentrates nutrients in the process streams. Phosphorus precipitates as struvite, ammonia nitrogen is stripped and captured, and potassium is recovered from the liquor.

Why does phosphorus recovery matter?

Phosphate rock is finite and most used phosphorus is lost to landfill, lagoons or waterways, where it drives algal blooms. Recovery turns that liability into fertilizer.

What makes struvite a good fertilizer?

It releases slowly as roots demand it, so nutrients feed the crop instead of leaching. It is granular, low-odour and blends well with biochar.

Who does this serve?

Utilities and processors who cut disposal and compliance costs, and blenders and growers who gain a local, traceable nutrient source.
