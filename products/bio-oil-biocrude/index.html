Renewable liquids, from waste to the refinery gate.

Bio-oil and biocrude are the renewable liquids Pyroltech produces from organic waste. Fast pyrolysis turns dry residues into bio-oil; hydrothermal liquefaction (HTL) turns wet streams like biosolids and food waste into a denser, more stable biocrude. Both are sold as low-carbon fuel and as co-processing feedstock for existing refineries, and both are the starting point for upgrading into drop-in renewable diesel, marine fuel and sustainable aviation fuel.

What is the difference between bio-oil and biocrude?

Pyrolysis bio-oil is the condensed liquid from fast pyrolysis of dry biomass such as forestry and agricultural residues. It is oxygen-rich, which makes it well suited to industrial heat and power and, with conditioning, to co-processing. HTL biocrude is made by hydrothermal liquefaction, which uses hot pressurised water to convert wet feedstocks, biosolids, sludges and food waste, without any drying step. The result is denser, lower in oxygen and closer in character to petroleum crude, which is exactly why refiners like it as a feedstock. Pyroltech builds both routes and matches the route to the feedstock at your site.

Why do refiners want a waste-derived liquid?

Fuel producers face rising low-carbon obligations, and co-processing is the cheapest first move because it uses the hydrotreaters and crackers they already own. A waste-derived liquid brings a low carbon intensity without competing with food crops, and it arrives as a pumpable feedstock rather than a solid to be handled. Pyroltech supplies to an agreed assay and works with the refiner on blend ratio, pre-treatment and logistics, so the barrier to starting is a trial volume, not a new unit.

How does it get to drop-in fuels?

Hydrotreating removes oxygen and stabilises the liquid into finished hydrocarbons. That upgrading step can happen inside an existing refinery through co-processing, or as a dedicated stage that turns bio-oil and biocrude into renewable diesel, marine fuel and naphtha and, on the certified aviation pathway, into sustainable aviation fuel. Selling the intermediate or upgrading it further is a commercial choice, and the plant supports both.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between bio-oil and biocrude?

Bio-oil comes from fast pyrolysis of dry biomass and is oxygen-rich, suited to heat, power and conditioned co-processing. Biocrude comes from HTL of wet feedstocks, is denser and lower in oxygen, and is closer to petroleum crude, which makes it a natural refinery feed.

Can refineries actually co-process these liquids?

Yes, in existing hydrotreaters and FCC units at modest blend ratios. We supply to an agreed specification and work with the refiner on blend level and pre-treatment.

How do they become drop-in fuels?

Through hydrotreating and upgrading, which remove oxygen and yield renewable diesel, marine fuel, naphtha and SAF. The step can run inside an existing refinery or as a dedicated stage.

What feedstocks work?

Dry residues such as forestry and agricultural waste go to pyrolysis bio-oil. Wet streams such as biosolids, sludges and food-processing waste go to HTL biocrude with no drying needed.
