Drop-in fuels, made from waste instead of crude.

Pyroltech renewable fuels are drop-in hydrocarbons made by upgrading the bio-oil and biocrude we produce from organic waste. The slate covers renewable diesel for road and off-road fleets, low-sulfur marine fuel for shipping, and renewable naphtha for gasoline blending and petrochemical feed. They meet the same specifications as their fossil equivalents, so nothing about the engine, tank or pipeline has to change. Sustainable aviation fuel belongs to the same family and has its own page.

Why fuels from waste rather than crops?

Most renewable fuel today leans on vegetable oils and crops, feedstocks that are capped by land and food-market pressure. Pyroltech starts from streams nobody wants: forestry and agricultural residues, biosolids, food-processing waste. That keeps the carbon intensity low, keeps the feedstock cost structurally cheap, often negative where tipping fees apply, and keeps the supply local to the plant. For fuel buyers under low-carbon fuel obligations, waste-derived fuel is the compliance tonne that does not raise food-versus-fuel questions.

How are they made?

The chain has two steps. First, pyrolysis or hydrothermal liquefaction converts waste into a renewable liquid, bio-oil or biocrude. Second, hydrotreating and upgrading remove oxygen and impurities and fractionate the result into finished diesel, marine fuel and naphtha cuts. The upgrading step can run in a dedicated unit or inside an existing refinery through co-processing, which is often the fastest route to first volumes. The underlying platform draws on 20+ years of engineering across three continents and runs commercially today, including a 2 MW plant in Turkey, running non-stop. Ask us for the details.

Who buys them?

Fleets and fuel distributors take renewable diesel to cut fleet emissions without touching their equipment. Shipping lines and bunker suppliers take marine fuel as carbon rules tighten on the water. Refiners and chemical producers take naphtha as a low-carbon blendstock and cracker feed. And airlines take the aviation cut as SAF. Each product has an established market and an established spec; our job is to land the right slate for the feedstock and the buyers around your site.

Frequently asked questions

What makes a fuel drop-in?

It meets the same specification as its fossil equivalent, so it runs in existing engines, pipelines and storage with no modification. Upgrading removes the oxygen and impurities that separate raw bio-liquids from finished fuel.

How is renewable diesel different from biodiesel?

Biodiesel is an ester (FAME) with blend limits and cold-weather constraints. Renewable diesel is a pure hydrocarbon, so it works at any blend level, up to 100 percent, in unmodified engines.

What feedstock do these fuels come from?

Waste-derived bio-oil and biocrude: forestry and agricultural residues, biosolids and food-processing streams. Low carbon intensity, no food-versus-fuel trade-off.

Does Pyroltech also make SAF?

Yes. SAF is the aviation cut of the same family, produced on certified pathways, with its own dedicated page.
