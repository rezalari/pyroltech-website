One material, engineered into many grades.

Biocarbon is the stable, carbon-rich solid produced when biomass is heated without oxygen. It is one material, but not one product. By tuning feedstock, temperature and residence time, Pyroltech engineers it into distinct grades: a reductant for steel, a soil amendment for farms, a base for activated carbon, and specialty carbons for concrete, remediation, animal husbandry and, increasingly, batteries. Every durable grade also locks in roughly 2.5 tonnes of CO2 per tonne, a second revenue line on the same tonne.

Why is one material worth so many markets?

The value of biocarbon is not the char itself, it is the specification. Fixed carbon, ash, sulfur, porosity, surface area and particle size can each be steered in the reactor, so the same platform serves a steel mill on Monday and a vineyard on Tuesday. That flexibility is what lets a plant follow the strongest local offtake instead of being captive to a single buyer, and it is why we treat biocarbon as a grade family rather than a commodity.

Metallurgical biocarbon

The flagship grade. Tuned for high fixed carbon, low ash and low sulfur, it drops into blast-furnace injection, coke blends, sinter, EAF recarburizing and ferroalloy smelting, replacing 20 to 30 percent of fossil coke and PCI coal without process redesign. With met coke benchmarked around 240 to 290 dollars per tonne, it competes on cost as well as carbon. Full spec and applications are on the metallurgical biocarbon page.

Soil-grade biochar

Run for porosity rather than density, soil-grade biochar holds water and nutrients in the root zone, gives soil microbes a durable habitat and keeps its carbon in the ground for centuries. It pairs naturally with wood vinegar from the same process and stacks credit-eligible carbon removal on every tonne applied. Details are on the biochar soil amendment page.

Activated carbon

Biocarbon is also the feedstock for our bio-based activated carbon. Steam or chemical activation opens the pore structure to surface areas above 1000 square metres per gram, a renewable alternative to coal-based and coconut-shell carbons in water, air and gas purification and in gold recovery. See the activated carbon page for grades and uses.

Construction and concrete

Fine biocarbon can be blended into concrete, mortars, asphalt and building boards as a lightweight carbon filler. The draw is twofold: it modifies material properties such as density and humidity behaviour, and it turns a building into long-term carbon storage, since the carbon stays locked in the structure for its whole service life. We develop these blends with producers against their own mix designs and standards.

Water and soil remediation

The same porosity that serves agriculture makes biocarbon an effective sorbent for remediation work: binding heavy metals, hydrocarbons and nutrient runoff in contaminated soils, stormwater systems and constructed wetlands. Remediation grades sit between soil biochar and activated carbon on the spectrum, giving project engineers a lower-cost media where full activation is not required.

Animal feed and bedding

Feed-grade charcoal has a long history in animal husbandry. Clean, well-specified biocarbon is used as a feed supplement to support digestion and as a bedding additive that manages moisture and odour in barns. After use, the carbon travels with the manure back onto fields, where it keeps working as a soil amendment, a rare example of a product with two useful lives.

Hard carbon for batteries

An emerging grade we are watching closely. Hard carbon derived from biomass is a candidate anode material for sodium-ion batteries, which do not rely on graphite the way lithium-ion cells do. It is early, and we say so plainly, but the raw material and the process discipline are the same ones we already run every day, so it is a natural extension of the platform for partners who want to develop it with us.

Frequently asked questions

What is biocarbon?

The stable, carbon-rich solid left when biomass is heated without oxygen. Feedstock and process conditions decide fixed carbon, ash, porosity and sizing, which is why one material becomes many grades.

What is the difference between biocarbon, biochar and charcoal?

Same family, named by use. Charcoal is fuel, biochar goes into soil, and biocarbon is the engineered industrial version, specified for jobs like replacing coke or being activated for filtration.

Which grade replaces metallurgical coke?

Metallurgical biocarbon, a drop-in for 20 to 30 percent of coke and PCI coal with a Scope 1 CO2 cut potential above 30 percent at full substitution.

Do biocarbon grades earn carbon-removal credits?

Durable-storage grades qualify, at roughly 2.5 tonnes of CO2 per tonne. Biochar carbon removal is an IPCC-recognised pathway, so the credit stacks on the product sale.

Where is production proven?
