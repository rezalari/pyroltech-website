How it works

Biomass carbon is normally released back to the atmosphere as it decays or burns. Pyrolysis converts it into stable biocarbon instead. Because the storage is durable and quantifiable, it qualifies as carbon dioxide removal under established methodologies, and the same biocarbon still performs its physical job in steel, soil or filtration.

Why it matters

Biochar carbon removal is one of the most credible and scalable durable-CDR pathways available today, and it has made up the large majority of delivered durable removals in recent years. For operators it turns decarbonization into income; for buyers it is a real, verifiable tonne rather than an avoidance claim.

Frequently asked questions

How does biocarbon create removal credits?

Pyrolysis locks labile biomass carbon into centuries-stable biocarbon. That durable, measurable storage is certifiable as removal, about 2.5 tonnes CO2 per tonne of biocarbon.

Can we sell the product and the credit?

Yes. The physical biocarbon is sold into its end use while the durable-carbon component is certified as a credit, so both stack.

Is it recognised?

Yes, biochar carbon removal is an IPCC-recognised pathway and the majority of delivered durable removals recently.
