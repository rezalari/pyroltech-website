How it works

From waste stream to revenue, in four steps.

Pyroltech turns organic and carbon-rich waste into sellable products and carbon-removal credits. The path is deliberately simple: we assess your feedstock, pilot the conversion on your actual material, deploy a unit sized to your site, portable or field-scale, then the plant operates while you sell the products and the credits. No step asks you to bet on the next one; each stage proves the case for the one after it.

What happens in the feedstock assessment?

Everything starts with what you actually have. You send a sample of the waste stream and tell us the volumes and seasonality; we analyse its composition and moisture, then map it to the right conversion route: pyrolysis for dry carbon-rich material, hydrothermal for wet streams, gasification when the target is fuels. The output is concrete: which products your stream yields, roughly what they earn at your location, and what the logistics look like. If the numbers do not work, we say so at this stage, not after steel is in the ground.

Why pilot before deploying?

Lab analysis predicts; a pilot run proves. Your material goes through the pilot process so yields, product quality and handling behaviour are confirmed on the real stream rather than on assumptions. Buyers of the end products get samples they can test against their own specifications, which is what turns interest into offtake. The pilot is the gate: capital decisions are made on measured results.

Portable or field-size: how is deployment sized?

Deployment follows the feedstock, not the other way around. Where residues are dispersed, forestry landings, remote communities, seasonal agricultural sites, a portable unit moves to the material and avoids hauling low-value waste over long distances. Where a steady stream arrives at one location, a mill, a treatment plant, a processing facility, a field-size plant is installed on site and scaled to the volumes you actually have, with headroom to grow.

Commercial structure is sized the same way. Depending on the site and the volumes, the model can be a straightforward equipment purchase, a joint venture, or a build-own-operate arrangement where Pyroltech runs the plant and you share the value. That conversation happens early, in the assessment stage.

Has this path been walked before?

Many times, over more than 20 years. The engineering behind Pyroltech has been built and operated across three continents: West Asia and the Middle East, from Turkey to Saudi Arabia and the UAE; Europe, from the Netherlands to Germany and Sweden; and East Asia, across Korea, Japan and China; now deployed in North America. The current reference is a 2 MW plant in Turkey, running non-stop. Ask us for details and we will take you through it.

Frequently asked questions

What does Pyroltech actually do with my waste?

We convert it on site into sellable products, biocarbon, bio-oil, wood vinegar, clean gases, plus durable carbon-removal credits. The waste stops being a disposal cost and becomes a revenue line.

Which feedstocks qualify?

Most dry carbon-rich streams suit pyrolysis: forestry and sawmill residues, agricultural residues, end-of-life wood, tires. Wet streams such as biosolids, food waste and manures go through the hydrothermal route, no drying needed.

Do I have to buy the unit?

Not necessarily. Models range from a paid study through equipment purchase to joint ventures and build-own-operate arrangements, matched to your site in the assessment stage.

What do I earn once it is running?

Product revenue plus credit-eligible carbon removal at roughly 2.5 t CO2 per tonne of biocarbon, with avoided disposal and hauling costs on top.

Is this proven technology?

Yes. More than 20 years of operating history across three continents, and a 2 MW plant in Turkey running non-stop today. Contact us for details.
