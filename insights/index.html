What do we write about?

Three threads run through everything here. First, where value hides in waste: which residues carry enough carbon, energy or nutrients to be worth converting, and which do not. Second, how the conversion works: pyrolysis, hydrothermal processing and gas upgrading, explained without jargon. Third, what the products earn: how biocarbon prices against met coke, how carbon-removal credits stack on a physical product, and why a tipping fee can be the strongest line in the model.

Why publish this at all?

Because the biggest barrier in this sector is not technology, it is unfamiliarity. Operators know their waste problem intimately but have no reason to know what a reductant spec or a removal registry is. Buyers know their carbon target but not what a pyrolysis plant can and cannot promise. Writing the answers down once, carefully, saves everyone the same first hour of every call, and lets the calls start at the interesting part.
