Which spec sheets can you request?

A current spec sheet exists for every product line. Tell us the product and the intended use, and we send the sheet with any application notes that matter for your case.

How does feedstock map to product?

The logic is simpler than it looks. What goes in largely decides what comes out; the process settings decide the split between solid, liquid and gas. Read each card as a row of the matrix: a feedstock family on the left, its natural products on the right. The exact yields for your material are what a feedstock assessment establishes.

Forestry & wildfire residueClean woody biomass. Strongest route to biocarbon and metallurgical biocarbon, with biochar, wood vinegar and bio-oil alongside.
Agriculture & food wasteStraws, shells, husks and processing residues. Biochar soil amendment, wood vinegar and clean gases, with activated carbon from selected shells.
Tires & rubberEnd-of-life tires. Recovered carbon black plus bio-oil and clean gases; carbon here is non-biogenic, so the value is in the products, not credits.
Biosolids & sludgeMunicipal and industrial sludges. Nutrient recovery, a stabilised carbon and clean gases, with contaminants destroyed in the process.
Seafood processing wasteShells and residues. Nutrient recovery and mineral-rich soil amendments.
Municipal plasticsSorted waste plastics. Bio-oil and biocrude routes toward renewable fuels, subject to feedstock quality.

Two rules of thumb hold across the matrix. Biogenic feedstocks can stack carbon-removal credits on top of the physical product, roughly 2.5 tonnes of CO2 per tonne of biocarbon. And hazardous or contaminated streams earn twice: once as a tipping or gate fee for destruction, once as product revenue.

What about white papers?

White papers on the core pathways, biocarbon in metallurgy, durable carbon removal, and feedstock economics, are in preparation. We would rather publish fewer documents that hold up than many that need caveats. If you want them on release, leave your details through the contact page and we will send each one as it lands. In the meantime, the technology pages explain the process itself, and the how it works page walks the full chain from feedstock to revenue.

Why route everything through contact?

Because a spec sheet without context creates more questions than it answers. When a request comes through the contact page, the sheet arrives with the assumptions spelled out and a person who can confirm what changes for your feedstock, your volumes and your site. It also means nothing on this site goes stale; the document you receive is always the current one.
