A 2 MW plant in Turkey, running non-stop.

This is the shortest possible proof of what we do: a 2 MW waste-to-energy plant in Turkey, running non-stop. Real, locally collected waste goes in one end. Grid electricity comes out the other. The plant powers its own operation from what it generates, and every megawatt-hour it exports displaces power that would otherwise come from a fossil-heavy grid. The rest of this page explains what that proves, and what we can show you under NDA.

What does the plant do?

The plant takes in waste as it actually arrives, variable, mixed and imperfect, and thermally converts it into energy. The electricity it produces is exported to the national grid as dependable power. There is no cherry-picked laboratory feedstock and no batch demonstration schedule. It is a commercial asset doing a daily job, continuously.

What goes in, and what comes out?

In: waste streams that the surrounding area needs to get rid of anyway. Out: grid electricity, plus the heat that keeps the conversion process running. The plant is a working example of the equation behind everything Pyroltech builds. A disposal cost on one side becomes an energy revenue on the other, and the material that would have been landfilled or openly burned is put to work instead.

How does it power itself?

A share of the energy released during conversion is fed back to run the plant's own systems. Once up and running, the process sustains itself from its own output, which is what makes non-stop operation economic rather than aspirational. The exported power is what is left after the plant has paid its own energy bill, so the figure that reaches the grid is a net, honest number.

What emissions does it avoid?

Two things happen at once. First, the waste no longer decomposes in a landfill or burns in the open, so those uncontrolled emissions are avoided. Second, every megawatt-hour the plant exports is a megawatt-hour the grid does not need to generate from fossil fuel. The plant earns its environmental case the same way it earns its commercial one, continuously, at the meter.

Why is the plant not named here?

The owner's commercial arrangements are confidential, and we respect that. What we publish openly is the shape of the proof: 2 MW, Turkey, real waste, non-stop, self-powered, grid-connected. What we share privately is everything else. The plant was delivered by a team that has been building energy and conversion plants for more than 20 years across three continents, and this is how established operators handle references: quietly, and in person.

What does it prove for your project?

Most waste-to-value proposals fail on the same three doubts: will it run on real waste, will it run continuously, and will the energy balance close. This plant answers all three at commercial scale. If you are evaluating a project for a municipality, a utility or an industrial site, the relevant question is no longer whether the technology works. It is how the same equation maps onto your waste stream, your grid and your economics, and that is a conversation we can ground in a running plant.
