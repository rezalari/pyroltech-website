### Architect notes

Overall this is a strong, GEO-aware IA: clear three-audience model (research / producers / users), buyer-question target_queries, and a deep product+industry taxonomy. The biggest structural gaps are (1) a missing FEEDSTOCK taxonomy axis (buyers search by material, not just industry) which is the single largest untapped programmatic-SEO surface; (2) missing infrastructure/trust pages standard for B2B cleantech (sitemap.xml, robots.txt, llms.txt, sustainability/LCA, certifications, news/press, investors, accessibility, glossary, resources/datasheets); and (3) missing conversion + comparison surfaces (quote, pricing/cost-factors, process/how-it-works, thank-you, and a /compare cluster the brief explicitly asked for). Top consistency risks: the orphaned /partners/research subtree (no /partners hub), role overlap between /solutions and /industries, several product-vs-industry cannibalization pairs (activated-carbon, carbon-removal, construction), placeholder 'example-*' slugs, and the lack of an explicit per-page-type schema template plus canonicalization/i18n policy. Fixing the schema template and internal-link mesh BEFORE generating the ~80 long-tail spokes is the highest-leverage move, because those rules are nearly free to apply at template time and very expensive to retrofit across 130+ pages. No em dashes were used anywhere in this output, per the hard rule.
