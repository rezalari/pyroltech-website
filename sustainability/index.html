Sustainability

Sustainability is the product, not the report.

The Pyroltech platform is sustainable in three measurable ways at once. It removes carbon durably, locking roughly 2.5 tonnes of CO2 per tonne of biocarbon into a form that lasts for centuries. It displaces fossil inputs, replacing 20-30% of metallurgical coke in steelmaking and fossil carbon in fuels, filtration and rubber. And it diverts waste that would otherwise be landfilled or burned. Every one of those claims is quantified, third-party verified and priced, because a sustainability claim that cannot survive an audit is not one we make.

How does the platform remove carbon?

Biomass carbon is borrowed carbon. When residues decay or burn, the CO2 the plants absorbed goes straight back to the atmosphere. Pyrolysis interrupts that cycle by converting the labile carbon into stable biocarbon that does not decompose on any timescale that matters, centuries rather than seasons. That is durable carbon dioxide removal, roughly 2.5 tonnes of CO2 locked away per tonne of biocarbon, and it is recognised by the IPCC as a removal pathway. Because the storage is measurable, it is certifiable, and because it is certifiable, it is bankable: removal credits have recently been indexed around $130-165 per tonne of CO2. The same tonne of biocarbon still does its physical job in a furnace, a filter or a field while the carbon inside it stays put.

How does it displace fossil inputs?

Removal is only half the ledger. The other half is what our products push out of the economy. Metallurgical biocarbon replaces 20-30% of the fossil coke and PCI coal a steel operation buys, with more than 30% Scope 1 CO2 cut potential at full substitution, and met coke is not cheap carbon to begin with at roughly $240-290 per tonne. Recovered carbon black substitutes virgin fossil carbon black in rubber and plastics. Renewable fuels, biocrude and clean gases displace their fossil equivalents in engines, refineries and turbines. Each displacement is an emission that never happens, on top of the carbon the biocarbon itself has already locked away.

How does it divert waste?

Every Pyroltech project starts with a material someone is paying to get rid of. Forestry slash, agricultural residues, end-of-life tires, biosolids, treated wood, seafood processing waste. Left alone, these streams cost money and emit anyway: landfills generate methane, slash piles are burned in the open, and disposal fees keep climbing. In British Columbia, tipping fees for treated wood alone run around $110-170 per tonne before hauling. Converting these streams turns a permanent cost into feedstock, shrinks what goes into the ground and replaces uncontrolled emissions with a controlled process whose carbon ends up in products instead of the sky.

What does lifecycle thinking mean here?

We count everything, not just the flattering parts. A credible claim has to hold across the whole chain: feedstock collection and hauling, process energy, conversion efficiency, product transport and end use. The platform is built to keep that ledger favourable by design. Plants run on their own process energy rather than imported power, the 2 MW plant in Turkey, running non-stop, powers its own operation. Feedstock is sourced close to where it is processed, and products ship into markets that already exist, so no parallel infrastructure has to be built and powered. Where a stream does not pencil out on a lifecycle basis, we say so and do not build it.

How is it certified and verified?

Certification is a section of its own because it is where sustainability claims either become assets or stay marketing. Our approach has three layers. First, quantification: each project's removals are calculated against an established biochar carbon-removal methodology, with the stability of the carbon and the full lifecycle accounted for. Second, independent verification: an accredited third party audits the numbers, we do not grade our own work. Third, registry issuance: verified removals are issued as certificates through a recognised registry, so a buyer holds a serialised, retirable instrument rather than a promise. Product-side claims follow the same discipline, specifications and biogenic content are documented per shipment, and the standards relevant to each end market are addressed project by project. Frequently asked questions

How does the platform remove carbon?

Pyrolysis converts biomass carbon that would otherwise return to the atmosphere into stable biocarbon lasting centuries, roughly 2.5 tonnes of CO2 per tonne, credit-eligible under the IPCC-recognised biochar pathway.

How does it displace fossil inputs?

Biocarbon replaces 20-30% of met coke in steelmaking with more than 30% Scope 1 cut potential, and other outputs displace fossil carbon black, fuels and gases in their own markets.

Is the removal certified?

Yes, per project: quantified against an established methodology, verified by an independent third party and issued through a recognised registry as certificates, not claims.

Does the model depend on subsidies?

No. It is built on real prices: avoided disposal costs, product sales into existing markets, and removal credits recently indexed around $130-165 per tonne of CO2.
