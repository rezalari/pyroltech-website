Technology / Gasification & upgrading

From waste to clean molecules.

Gasification converts carbon-rich waste at high temperature, with a controlled and deliberately restricted supply of oxygen or steam, into syngas: a mixture of hydrogen and carbon monoxide. Syngas is the universal building block of the fuels and chemicals industry, and once it is cleaned it can be upgraded into hydrogen, renewable natural gas, methanol, ammonia, Fischer-Tropsch fuels and sustainable aviation fuel. Waste goes in; refinery-grade molecules come out.

What is gasification?

Gasification sits between pyrolysis and combustion. Where pyrolysis excludes oxygen entirely to preserve solid carbon, and combustion supplies excess oxygen to burn everything, gasification meters in just enough oxygen or steam at high temperature to transform the feedstock into gas without fully burning it. The carbon and hydrogen in the waste survive, re-formed as syngas.

That distinction is the whole business case. An incinerator recovers heat and leaves you with ash and flue gas. A gasifier hands you a chemical feedstock that the fuels and chemicals industry has been building on for a century. The value per tonne of waste is a different category.

What is syngas, and why does it matter?

Syngas is a mixture of hydrogen and carbon monoxide, with the ratio tunable by how the gasifier is run. It matters because it is a junction point: from the same cleaned gas, established catalytic processes branch out to almost every clean molecule the energy transition needs. The chemistry downstream of syngas is not experimental. Methanol synthesis, ammonia synthesis, methanation and Fischer-Tropsch are mature industrial processes; what changes with Pyroltech is the origin of the carbon, from fossil resources to waste.

Before upgrading, the raw gas is cleaned: particulates, tars and contaminants are removed so the gas meets the specification of the downstream catalyst. Gas cleaning is where gasification projects are won or lost, and it is engineered into the flowsheet from day one, not bolted on.

When is gasification the right route?

The choice between routes follows the target product. If the goal is solid carbon, biocarbon for steel, filtration or soil, pyrolysis is the workhorse because it preserves the carbon skeleton. If the goal is gases or liquid fuels, gasification converts nearly all of the carbon in the feedstock into syngas and gives the fuels train the most material to work with. Wet streams point to the hydrothermal route instead.

Many projects assess two routes against the same feedstock and let the offtake decide. The comparison is laid out in plain terms in pyrolysis vs gasification.

Frequently asked questions

What is gasification?

High-temperature conversion of carbon-rich waste with a controlled, restricted amount of oxygen or steam. The material transforms into syngas, a hydrogen and carbon monoxide mixture, rather than burning to ash.

How is it different from incineration?

Incineration burns waste completely and recovers only heat. Gasification starves the reaction of oxygen so the carbon and hydrogen survive as a usable chemical feedstock, worth far more per tonne.

What can syngas be upgraded into?

Hydrogen, renewable natural gas, methanol, ammonia, and Fischer-Tropsch fuels including renewable diesel and sustainable aviation fuel. All are established industrial routes.

When gasification instead of pyrolysis?

Pyrolysis when the target is solid carbon products; gasification when the target is gases or liquid fuels and you want maximum carbon into the syngas. We assess both against your feedstock.
