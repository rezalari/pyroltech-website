Who are we looking for?

Three kinds of people, at any level of seniority. What they share is a bias for finishing: the plant either runs or it does not, and we like colleagues who find that clarifying rather than stressful.

EngineeringProcess, mechanical and controls engineers who design, model and de-risk pyrolysis systems, from feedstock handling to gas cleanup.
OperationsPeople who commission, run and maintain plants, and who can manage feedstock logistics, quality and safety day after day.
PartnershipsBuilders of relationships with feedstock owners, product offtakers, researchers and government, who quantify a problem before pitching a solution.

How do we work?

Deliberately small in scope, deliberately high in standard. We start projects from zero, keep them tightly focused, and grow them from proof rather than projection. Claims are measured, numbers are verified, and nobody oversells. The engineering lineage behind that culture was built across three continents: West Asia and the Middle East, from Turkey to Saudi Arabia and the UAE; Europe, from the Netherlands to Germany and Sweden; and East Asia, across Korea, Japan and China; now deployed in North America.

Day to day that means short lines of communication, direct access to the people who designed the equipment, and the expectation that whatever you own, you own completely.

How do you apply?

Email info@pyroltech.com with the subject line "Careers". Send a short note: who you are, the most concrete thing you have built or run, and why waste-to-value is where you want to spend your energy. A link or a few pages of evidence beats a long CV. We read everything and reply to anyone whose note shows they read this page.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need pyrolysis experience?

No. We need strong fundamentals in your discipline and the willingness to learn the specifics on real equipment. The technology can be taught; the habit of finishing things cannot.

Is the work remote or on-site?

Operations roles are on-site by nature. Engineering and partnerships roles are flexible, with travel to sites when a project needs eyes on the hardware. We are anchored in North America.

Do you take students or recent graduates?

Sometimes, usually through our university research collaborations. If you work on pyrolysis, carbon materials or waste valorization, see research partners or write to us directly.
