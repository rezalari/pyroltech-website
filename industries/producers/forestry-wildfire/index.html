Why is forest residue a liability today?

Every harvest, mill shift and fuel-mitigation treatment produces biomass that has nowhere good to go. Slash left in the forest is standing wildfire fuel. Burning it in piles releases the carbon and the smoke while returning nothing. Hauling it out costs more than the fibre is worth, and where waste wood does reach a facility, tipping in British Columbia runs about 110 to 170 dollars per tonne for treated and waste wood before hauling is even counted. The material is a cost, a hazard and an emission all at once.

What do we turn it into?

Pyrolysis converts woody residue into biocarbon, a stable, high-carbon product with real markets, plus process heat that can dry feedstock or serve nearby loads. Because the carbon is locked into a form that stays stable for centuries, each tonne of biocarbon also generates roughly 2.5 tonnes of credit-eligible carbon removal under an IPCC-recognised pathway. The same pile that would have been burned becomes two revenue streams.

How do portable units work at the roadside?

Hauling wet, low-density residue is what kills most biomass projects, so Pyroltech brings the unit to the biomass instead. Portable systems are set up at the roadside, landing or mill yard, run on the residue that is already piled there, and produce a dense, dry biocarbon that is worth trucking. When a cut block or treatment area is finished, the unit moves. No permanent civil works, no long-term feedstock contracts for fibre that will not be there next season.

Where is this running today?

The technology is commercial, not conceptual. Pyroltech's team has built waste-to-value plants for more than 20 years across three continents, and the core system runs today at a 2 MW plant in Turkey, running non-stop. Ask us for the details.

Why now?

Wildfire seasons are getting longer, fuel-mitigation budgets are growing, and open burning is under increasing restriction. At the same time, buyers are paying real money for durable carbon removal and for renewable carbon in industry. The residue is already being cut, piled and paid for. The only question is whether it goes up in smoke or into product.

Frequently asked questions

What forest residues can you process?

Slash and landing piles, bark, chips, sawmill residues such as sawdust, shavings and offcuts, and biomass from wildfire fuel-mitigation and thinning work.

Do we have to haul the residue to a plant?

No. Portable units work at the roadside or landing, so residue is converted close to where it is piled instead of hauled or burned.

What does the residue become?

Biocarbon for metallurgical, filtration and soil markets, process heat, and durable carbon-removal credits at roughly 2.5 t CO2 per tonne of biocarbon.

Is this better than open burning?

Yes. Burning releases the carbon and the smoke and returns nothing. Pyrolysis removes the same fire hazard while locking the carbon into product and credits.

What does disposal cost today?

As context, treated and waste-wood tipping in BC runs about $110-170 per tonne before hauling. Conversion turns that cost line into a revenue line.
