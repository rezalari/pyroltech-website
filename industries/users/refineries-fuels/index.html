Why is co-processing the pragmatic route?

Renewable-fuel obligations are tightening across North America and Europe, and the penalties for falling short are priced in real money. Building a standalone renewable-fuels plant is a decade-scale capital decision. Co-processing is not: it inserts biogenic feed into units that are already permitted, staffed and depreciated. The constraint has never been the concept, it has been a reliable supply of biogenic oil at a specification a refinery can actually run. That supply is our job.

What do we supply, and where does it go?

Pyroltech converts waste biomass and residues into bio-oil and biocrude through pyrolysis and hydrothermal processing. The hydrothermal route in particular yields a lower-oxygen, more energy-dense biocrude suited to hydrotreating, while stabilised pyrolysis oil suits FCC insertion. Every parcel is produced to an agreed specification, because a co-processing program lives or dies on feed consistency.

Has co-processing been proven?

Yes, at commercial refineries. Co-processing of biogenic oils in FCC and hydrotreating units is already practiced at operating refineries in Europe and North America, and the certification schemes that credit the renewable content are established. On the supply side, our conversion technology is no pilot claim: the reference is a 2 MW plant in Turkey, running non-stop. Ask us for the details in a call.

Why now?

Every compliance year that passes without renewable barrels is paid for in credits bought from someone else. Starting with a trial parcel is deliberately small: your engineers verify feed behaviour, corrosion and product quality on your own unit before any volume commitment. The refiners who secure waste-based feedstock supply early will be the ones setting terms later, because feedstock, not processing capacity, is the scarce end of this market.

Frequently asked questions

What is biocrude co-processing?

Feeding a controlled percentage of biogenic oil alongside fossil feed in your FCC or hydrotreater. Renewable carbon flows through existing hardware and the finished fuels carry certified renewable content.

Will bio-oil meet our feed spec?

Raw pyrolysis oil will not, which is why we supply stabilised and upgraded grades. The hydrothermal route yields a lower-oxygen biocrude suited to hydrotreating. Spec and insertion point are agreed with your engineers first.

Where does waste-based hydrogen fit?

Our clean gas stream can be reformed into hydrogen made from waste rather than natural gas, cutting the carbon intensity of the hydrotreating step as well as the feed.

How do volumes and logistics work?

Modular plants sited near feedstock mean supply starts with trial parcels and scales to steady contracted offtake, with spec, delivery mode and ramp-up set in the agreement.
