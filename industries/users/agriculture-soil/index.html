The problem

Working soils lose organic matter, drain nutrients and struggle to hold water, so growers spend more on fertiliser and irrigation for the same or falling yields. Conventional inputs feed the crop but do little for long-term soil structure, and most of the carbon added as raw organic matter breaks down and returns to the atmosphere within a season or two.

Our solution

Pyroltech turns clean biomass into a stable, porous biochar soil amendment that behaves like a reservoir in the root zone, holding moisture and nutrients where the plant can use them and hosting the microbes that keep soil alive. The wood vinegar captured in the same process adds an organic biostimulant. Because the carbon in biochar is stable, it stays in the ground for centuries instead of decaying, so it improves the soil and locks a durable, credit-eligible removal at once.

Why now

Input costs and water pressure keep rising, and buyers increasingly reward growers who can show real soil carbon rather than a claim. Biochar is one of the few inputs that improves the field today and produces a durable, verifiable removal, so a soil program can pay back through better yield, lower input spend and a second revenue line from carbon at the same time.

Frequently asked questions

What does biochar do for soil?

It is a porous, stable carbon that holds water and nutrients in the root zone, buffers pH and hosts soil microbes, so crops perform better and the carbon stays locked for centuries.

How is wood vinegar used?

The condensate from pyrolysis, diluted, is applied as a foliar and soil biostimulant through normal spray systems and pairs naturally with biochar.

Does soil biochar earn credits?

Yes. Applying durable biochar to soil is an IPCC-recognised removal pathway, about 2.5 t CO2 per tonne of biocarbon, so the same material can generate a credit-eligible removal.

Will it fit my operation?

Yes. Biochar spreads with standard equipment and blends into compost, manure or fertiliser; wood vinegar applies through normal sprayers. We tune size and blends to your crop.
