The problem

Grids need firm, dispatchable capacity, but the low-carbon options are often intermittent or far from where the load sits. At the same time, forestry and agricultural residues pile up and are burned openly or landfilled, wasting energy and releasing carbon. Utilities are left choosing between reliability and emissions, and paying to manage waste on top.

Our solution

Pyroltech thermal conversion turns those same residues into clean combustible gases that run engines, boilers or turbines for heat and power, or are upgraded into renewable natural gas for the pipeline. The energy is dispatchable and made from waste you already have nearby. Because the process also fixes part of the carbon as stable biocarbon, every project delivers energy and a durable, credit-eligible carbon product together.

Proof

It is already running. A 2 MW plant in Turkey operates non-stop, and our team has delivered energy-from-waste systems on three continents over more than two decades, now deployed in North America. The technology is proven and in service; ask us for the operating details and we will walk you through them.

Why now

Firm renewable capacity, waste-diversion pressure and rising fuel costs are all pushing utilities toward local, dispatchable energy. Turning residues into power, gas and heat cuts emissions on the fuel and the waste side at once, and the durable biocarbon co-product adds a carbon-removal revenue line that a straight burn cannot match.

Frequently asked questions

How does it turn waste into energy?

Pyrolysis and gasification break residues into clean gases that run engines, boilers or turbines, or upgrade into RNG, while leaving a stable biocarbon, so you get energy and a durable carbon product together.

Is it proven at scale?

Yes. We operate a 2 MW plant in Turkey running non-stop, with more than 20 years of projects across three continents. Contact us for the operating details.

What feedstocks work?

Forestry and agricultural residues, wood waste and other dry organic streams that would otherwise be burned openly, landfilled or left to decay.

How does it help us decarbonize?

Dispatchable renewable energy from local waste instead of fossil fuel, residue diversion, and durable carbon removal alongside the energy.
