The problem

Primary steelmaking depends on fossil coke and PCI coal as the reductant and carbon source, which makes carbon the core of the sector's emissions. Carbon pricing, customer Scope 3 targets and green-steel premiums all push in the same direction, but ripping out the blast furnace is neither fast nor cheap.

Our solution

Renewable metallurgical biocarbon behaves like coke as a reductant and carbon source, so it drops into the carbon you already use. Because it is biogenic, every tonne that replaces coke takes the matching fossil CO2 off your books, and the durable carbon it locks in is credit-eligible.

Proof

It is already being trialled. A leading British Columbia smelter ran a 500,558 dollar CleanBC-funded biocarbon trial replacing metallurgical coal and coke, and Canada's NRCan CanmetENERGY Bio-Coke program has demonstrated charcoal in coke blends. The pathway is proven; the work now is matching spec and volume to your furnace.

Why now

Carbon costs and green-steel demand are both climbing, and biocarbon is one of the few levers that cuts emissions inside the existing asset base today. Starting with a 20 to 30 percent blend lets you decarbonize and learn without betting the plant on a single new technology.

Frequently asked questions

How does biocarbon decarbonize steelmaking?

It replaces fossil coke and PCI with biogenic carbon, so the matching Scope 1 CO2 is removed, up to a 30 percent cut at full substitution, without changing the process.

How much can we replace?

Typically 20 to 30 percent across PCI, coke blend, sinter, EAF and ferroalloy or foundry use, without redesign. Higher with trials.

Will it meet our spec?

Yes. We tune feedstock and pyrolysis to your fixed carbon, ash, sulfur and sizing requirements.

Is there upside beyond compliance?

Yes. You displace purchased coke and earn credit-eligible carbon removal (about 2.5 t CO2 per tonne), while meeting buyer Scope 3 demand.
