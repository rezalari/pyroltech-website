Why is fuel the whole game for aviation and shipping?

Other sectors can electrify. Long-haul aircraft and deep-sea vessels cannot, not on any timeline their fleets are built around. Meanwhile SAF blending mandates are phasing in across major jurisdictions, shipping's emissions rules are tightening at the international level, and cargo customers are pushing their freight emissions upstream onto carriers. Both sectors face the same equation: the asset stays, so the fuel must change. The bottleneck is credible, waste-based supply.

What do we offer airlines and shipping lines?

Our platform turns residues into biocrude and clean gases, the two intermediates behind today's certified low-carbon fuel routes. Biocrude upgrades into SAF blendstock and renewable diesel range fuels. Clean gases are the synthesis building block for green methanol, the fuel the dual-fuel shipping fleet is being built for. Because the feedstock is waste, the lifecycle case stands up to the scrutiny regulators and customers now apply.

Are these pathways real or still on paper?

The routes are certified and flying. Waste-derived SAF pathways are approved for blending under international jet-fuel standards and are already in commercial use, and methanol-fuelled vessels are entering service with major carriers. On the conversion side, our reference is a 2 MW plant in Turkey, running non-stop. Ask us for the details in a call.

Why now?

Blending mandates arrive on the calendar whether supply is secured or not, and the airlines and carriers contracting waste-based volumes early are the ones who will not be buying at the margin later. Feedstock, not demand, is the scarce end of this market. Starting the conversation now means pathway, specification and volumes are settled before the obligation bites, not after.

Frequently asked questions

How does waste become jet fuel?

Pyrolysis and hydrothermal processing turn residues into biocrude and clean gases, which are upgraded through established routes into SAF blendstock meeting international blending standards. It drops into existing aircraft and airport infrastructure.

What can shipping buy today?

Marine biofuel blends that drop into conventional engines with no retrofit, and green methanol pathways for the dual-fuel fleet, synthesised from our clean gas stream.

Why waste feedstock rather than crops?

No food-versus-fuel exposure, no agricultural price risk, and a stronger lifecycle profile, which is the direction aviation and marine fuel regulation is moving.

How do we start?

A conversation about routes, volumes and timelines. We map feedstock and pathway to your demand and structure offtake that starts small and scales with your obligations.
