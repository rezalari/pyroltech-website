Why is kiln fuel the place to start?

A cement kiln's CO2 comes from two places: the chemistry of calcination and the fuel that heats it. The chemistry is hard to change quickly. The fuel is not. Coal and petcoke are among the most emissions-intensive fuels a plant can burn, and carbon pricing, customer demand for lower-carbon cement and corporate net-zero commitments are all converging on the fuel line. Waiting for kiln-scale carbon capture means paying for every tonne in the meantime.

How does biocarbon co-firing work?

Renewable biocarbon is a dense, low-moisture, high-heating-value solid fuel made by pyrolysing waste biomass. It mills, conveys and doses like the solid fuel you already handle, so it enters the existing fuel train rather than a new one. Because its carbon is biogenic, every tonne that displaces coal or petcoke takes the matching fossil CO2 off your Scope 1 inventory. Blend rates start low and ramp in controlled steps as the trial data comes in.

Is anyone actually doing this?

Major producers are already trialling it. Leading cement makers in Europe and North America are running biocarbon co-firing programs in precalciners and main burners, and lime producers are following the same path. The underlying technology is not a pilot claim for us either: our reference plant is a 2 MW plant in Turkey, running non-stop. Ask us for the details in a call.

Why now?

Carbon costs on kiln fuel only move one way, and low-carbon cement is becoming a procurement requirement rather than a marketing line. Co-firing biocarbon is one of the few levers that cuts emissions inside the existing kiln today, at a pace you control. Starting with a modest blend lets your operations team build confidence and data while the savings accumulate, instead of waiting for a single large capital project to solve everything at once.

Frequently asked questions

Can biocarbon really drop into our kiln?

Yes. It mills, conveys and doses like the coal and petcoke you already fire, in the precalciner and the main burner, with blend rates ramped in controlled steps during a trial.

How much fuel CO2 can we cut?

Every tonne of coal or petcoke displaced takes the matching fossil CO2 off your Scope 1 books, because the carbon is biogenic. The achievable blend rate is confirmed in your trial.

Will it affect clinker or lime quality?

Biocarbon ash is low and mineral-compatible, and is incorporated into the clinker like other solid-fuel ash. We tune fixed carbon, ash and sizing to your burner spec first.

Do we earn carbon-removal credits on kiln fuel?

Not on combusted fuel. The Scope 1 benefit comes from biogenic substitution. Removal credits, about 2.5 t CO2 per tonne, apply where biocarbon is durably stored instead of burned, and we can help you evaluate both routes.
