SAF vs renewable diesel.

The short verdict: these are sibling fuels from similar renewable barrels, and the real choice is about your buyer, not your chemistry. Renewable diesel meets road and marine diesel standards, works unblended in existing engines, and has the broader, simpler offtake today. Sustainable aviation fuel is the jet-range cut, certified to strict blending standards, and it is what airlines under mandates must buy. If your offtake is a fleet, ship or mine, go renewable diesel. If it is an airline, go SAF. Many plants make both.

Why does aviation get its own fuel?

Because an aircraft cannot pull over. Jet fuel operates across extremes of temperature and pressure with zero tolerance for failure, so anything blended into it is certified pathway by pathway before a drop is loaded. That rigour is why SAF qualification takes longer, and also why SAF demand is so durable: aviation cannot electrify long-haul flight on any near horizon, so mandated SAF volumes are effectively a structural buyer looking for supply.

Why is renewable diesel the pragmatic first sale?

Because the buyers already exist and the engines already run on it. Renewable diesel certifies against the diesel standards fleets use today, needs no blending wall, and sells into trucking, marine, rail and mining under low-carbon fuel programs that are live now. For a project that needs bankable offtake early, the diesel-range barrel is usually the anchor contract, with SAF layered on as the jet-range cut completes qualification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between SAF and renewable diesel?

Both are drop-in renewable hydrocarbons, often from the same upgraded barrel. SAF is the jet-range cut certified to jet blending standards; renewable diesel meets road and marine standards and runs unblended in existing engines.

Which fuel is easier to sell today?

Renewable diesel, with broader established offtake and a simpler certification path. SAF has strong mandate-driven demand but a longer qualification process.

Can one plant produce both?

Yes. Upgrading yields a range of cuts, so a project can sell diesel-range fuel from day one and add the jet-range cut as SAF once certified.

Which should anchor the business case?

The buyer you can contract. Airline or airport-linked offtake favours SAF; fleet, marine or industrial offtake favours renewable diesel. Many projects anchor on diesel and layer SAF in.
