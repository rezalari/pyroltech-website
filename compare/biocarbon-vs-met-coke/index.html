Biocarbon vs metallurgical coke.

The short verdict: biocarbon is a drop-in replacement for 20 to 30 percent of the met coke and PCI coal you buy today, with no process redesign. It matches coke as a reductant and carbon source, removes the corresponding fossil CO2 from your Scope 1 ledger, and adds a second revenue line of roughly 2.5 tonnes of credit-eligible CO2 removal per tonne. Coke keeps one clear advantage, bulk mechanical strength for full blast-furnace burden support. So the answer is a blend, not a swap.

Where does biocarbon win?

Anywhere the carbon is consumed rather than load-bearing. Pulverized coal injection, coke-oven blending, iron-ore sinter, EAF recarburising, ferroalloy and foundry duties all accept biocarbon at 20 to 30 percent today, tuned to the existing fixed-carbon, ash, sulfur and sizing spec. On those tonnes you stop buying fossil CO2, and the durable carbon you lock in earns credits on top of the product itself.

Where does met coke still win?

Burden support. A blast furnace needs a permeable column of strong lump carbon, and coke's mechanical strength is the reason it has held that job for two centuries. Biocarbon is not yet asked to carry that load alone, which is why the credible near-term strategy is substitution inside the blend, not a wholesale swap. That is also what makes the switch low-risk: nothing about the process changes.

What does the switch actually cost?

Less than the sticker comparison suggests. Met coke benchmarks around 240 to 290 dollars per tonne before carbon costs. Biocarbon is priced against that benchmark, and once you count the Scope 1 tonnes it removes, the customer Scope 3 demand it satisfies, and roughly 2.5 tonnes of credit-eligible CO2 removal per tonne, the effective cost of the blended tonnes drops. The right way to settle it is a furnace-spec quote against your current coke contract.

Frequently asked questions

Can biocarbon fully replace met coke?

Not across every duty today. It is a proven drop-in for 20 to 30 percent of coke and PCI without redesign; full burden support still relies on coke's strength, so the practical strategy is a blend.

Is biocarbon cheaper than met coke?

Coke benchmarks around $240-290 per tonne. Biocarbon is priced against that, and the comparison shifts in its favour once carbon costs and ~2.5 t CO2 per tonne of credit-eligible removal are counted.

How much CO2 does switching save?

Every displaced tonne of coke comes off Scope 1, with a cut of more than 30 percent possible at full substitution potential, and the durable carbon is credit-eligible.

Do we need to modify the furnace?

No. At 20 to 30 percent blends biocarbon drops into PCI, coke blends, sinter, EAF and ferroalloy duties, tuned to your existing spec.
